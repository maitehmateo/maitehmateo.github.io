5000 Nicaraguans lose their Temporaty Residency Permit

LEDE O SUMMARY

By Maite H. Mateo

An estimated 325,000 migrants from 13 TPS-designated countries resided in the United States

On Monday November 06, the Department of Homeland Security announced that has decided to terminate the TPS, Temporary Protected Status for Nicaragua with a 12-month delay. DHS also said that has not been able to reach a decision on Honduras. At the end of that six-month window, the homeland security secretary will make a decision to terminate or further extend the status.

The US government granted the TPS to Honduras and Nicaragua after the devastating hurricane Mitch in 1999. On November 6, in a statement issued by the Secretary of Homeland Security, Eliane Duke, announced the decision to end the TPS for 5,600 Nicaraguans , alleging that Daniel Ortega's government did not formally request the need for an extension. He also confirmed the postponement of his decision on 57,600 Hondurans to whom they provide a six-month extension.

TPS beneficiaries from El Salvador, Honduras, and Haiti reveals hardworking populations with strong family and other ties to the United States. In addition, high percentages have lived in the United States for 20 years or more, arrived as children, and have US citizen children.

The labor force participation rate of the TPS population from the three nations ranges from 81 to 88 %, which is well above the rate for the total US population is 63% and the foreign-born population is 66%.
